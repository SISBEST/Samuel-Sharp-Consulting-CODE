Prometheus: Analytical Mini-Essay

Samuel Sharp

In the past, myths were used to explain the origins of man, the world, and nature. Today, they are mainly used to teach life lessons. All myths have key elements and themes. In the myth of Prometheus, there are two key elements with the theme of power abuse.

The two key elements in Prometheus are supernatural or non-human characters and origins of life and the natural world. The first key element, supernatural or non-human characters, is demonstrated by the quote “...one of the mightiest Titans…” This quote shows that this story involves supernatural characters like Zeus, Prometheus, and the Titans. the second key element, origins of life and the natural world , is demonstrated by the quote “...and the first Man...” this quote shows that this story explains the creation of Man. These two quotes and elements show that Prometheus is a classic myth.

An important theme in the myth of Prometheus is abuse of power. A detail that shows this is “...punish Prometheus for deceiving him.” This quote shows Zeus punishing Prometheus for helping Man, which is abusing your power. Prometheus is helping Man! Why is he being punished? Another quote that demonstrates abuse of power is “...chained Prometheus to the top of a mountain.” This is extreme punishment (a bird eating your liver every day) , even for Greek myths. This also shows Zeus is abusing his power by punishing Prometheus so harshly.

The key element supernatural or non-human characters matches the theme abuse of power because Zeus (a supernatural or non-human character) is punishing Prometheus very harshly (abuse of power). Long ago, myths taught how the world worked. Now, they teach important skills. Myths play a large part in our lives. They’re even taught in schools (how this “mini”-essay came about). What do you think about myths? What role do they play in your lives?

-Samuel Sharp 2018
