HeLa Cells: Position Paragraph

By Samuel Sharp

Have you ever wondered how scientists have helped sick people? The polio vaccine and many other cures and treatments were developed using HeLa, or immortal cancer, cells. I think HeLa cells should still be used for research, even with the ethical issues surrounding them,         because the use was and is legal, people are making money off of culturing HeLa cells, and because they have been used to find cures and treatments. First, the use is legal. US law states that the doctor who removes body parts owns them. HeLa cells were removed first by George Gey. Therefore, they are his property. Henrietta Lacks does not decide what Gey does with his property. He was giving them out for free because he “saw the benefits for medical research and for humanity,” says Anh-Thi Tang, writer of “Immortal Cancer Cells”. Secondly, companies make huge money off of HeLa cells. This helps the economy grow. Lastly, cures, treatments, and vaccines have been made using HeLa cells, including the polio vaccine, developed by Jonas Salk. For these reasons, Hela cells should still be used for research.
